# OrgAdmin

## Who this role is for
Organization administrators who manage policies, org settings, and compliance workflows.

## What you see in the app
- `Policies` list and policy detail pages.
- `Templates` (view and preview).
- `Org Profile`, `Business Units`, `Exports`, `Reports`, `Audit & Logs`.

## Key responsibilities
- Create and manage policies for the organization.
- Maintain org profile and business unit structure.
- Monitor compliance status and audit activity.

## Common actions and how to do them

### Create a new policy
1) Open `Policies`.
2) Click `New Policy`.
3) Choose a template and fill in required fields.
4) Save the policy as draft.

### Create multiple policies from templates
1) Open `Policies`.
2) Choose bulk creation if available.
3) Select the templates to generate.
4) Confirm the framework and variable overrides.
5) Create the drafts.

### Edit a policy
1) Open `Policies`.
2) Select a policy to open its detail page.
3) Make edits in the policy content or metadata.
4) Save changes to create a new version.

### Submit a policy for review
1) Open the policy detail page.
2) Confirm the policy is ready.
3) Click `Submit for Review`.

### Respond to requested changes
1) Open the policy detail page.
2) Review the request comments.
3) Update the policy content or metadata.
4) Save a new version.
5) Submit for review again.

### Assign policies to business units
1) Open `Business Units`.
2) Select a business unit.
3) Assign policies for acknowledgment if supported.
4) Set due dates and confirm assignments.

### Export approved policies
1) Open `Exports`.
2) Choose a single policy export or a policy pack export.
3) Download the generated file when ready.

### Manage business units
1) Open `Business Units`.
2) Create or update business units.
3) Assign owners or update status as needed.

### Update the org policy profile
1) Open `Org Profile`.
2) Update organization size, industry, tone, and data types.
3) Save changes.

### Review compliance reports
1) Open `Reports`.
2) Review the compliance summary.
3) Check overdue assignments and overdue reviews.
4) Drill into a policy or user for detail.

### Audit activity for a policy
1) Open `Audit & Logs`.
2) Filter by policy name, action, or date range.
3) Review timeline entries for the policy lifecycle.

## Limits
- You cannot approve policies unless you also have the `PolicyApprover` role.
- You can view templates but only `SuperAdmin` can create, edit, or delete templates.
